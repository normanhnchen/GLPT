# Depth of Field

Depth of Field (DOF) or defocus blur occurs in real cameras because they need a large hole to gather light. A large hole causes blur to everything, so a lens in front of the film will allow focus at a certain distance. This distance from the camera to the plane where everything is in focus, is called the *focus distance*. The *aperture* controls the size of the lens and thus controls the blur factor.

## Thin Lens Approximation

In ray tracing, this approximation works by samples rays from an infinitely thin circular disk acting as the lens and sending them towards a point of focus.
